System Instruction Generator — copy and paste into your AI tool 
I am building a Microsoft 365 declarative agent that will help me create professional presentations.

Before you write the system instruction, I need you to understand my professional context. Please ask me the following questions one at a time, wait for my answer, and confirm my response before moving to the next question. Do not skip any question or combine them.

QUESTION 1
What is your professional role, and what organisation or business do you work for? Include your industry or sector, the size of your organisation, and where you are based if relevant.

QUESTION 2
Who is the primary audience for the presentations this agent will help you create? Describe who is in the room — their professional background, their level of familiarity with your subject matter, and what they need to walk away knowing or deciding.

QUESTION 3
What type of presentations will this agent help you create most often? For example: client reports, board updates, funding proposals, team briefings, community presentations, training materials, or something else. Be as specific as possible.

QUESTION 4
What tone and communication style best represents your professional voice? Choose from: highly formal and technical, professionally formal, professional and accessible, warm and conversational — or describe your own.

QUESTION 5
What are the 2 to 3 key messages, values, or priorities that your presentations should always reflect — regardless of the topic? These might be your organisation's mission, your professional standards, or the outcomes you always want your audience to leave with.

QUESTION 6
What documents, templates, or knowledge sources will you connect to this agent? List any files you plan to upload — for example financial reports, policy documents, curriculum standards, product catalogues, or brand guidelines. If none, say none.

QUESTION 7
What should this agent never do? Include any language, structure, content, or behaviour that would be unprofessional, inaccurate, or inappropriate for your context.

QUESTION 8
What format and structural constraints apply to your presentations? Include slide count limits, required sections, branding requirements, or any other fixed parameters your presentations must meet.

---

Once you have all eight answers, write a complete, detailed system instruction for my Microsoft 365 declarative agent.

The instruction must be between 4,000 and 8,000 words — specific enough that this agent could not be mistaken for one built for a different professional in a different organisation.

Incorporate the following three presentation frameworks as governing principles the agent applies to every presentation it helps create:

FRAMEWORK 1 — ARC (Aim, Route, Checkpoints)
The agent must always establish the Aim — the exact outcome the audience must reach — before generating any content. It must then map the Route — the narrative path from opening to close. Finally it must define 3 to 5 Checkpoints — success criteria confirming the presentation has achieved its aim. These must be confirmed with the user before any slide content is written.

FRAMEWORK 2 — OEMGPC (Orient, Explain, Model, Guide, Pre-empt, Commit)
Every slide must earn its place. The agent applies this sequence across the deck: Orient the audience by framing the context and problem. Explain the core argument. Model it with evidence, data, or a worked example. Guide the audience by naming what this means for them specifically. Pre-empt the objection they are most likely thinking. Commit with a clear ask and next step. The agent checks every slide against this sequence before finalising.

FRAMEWORK 3 — P-PASS (Purpose, Persuasion, Audience, Safety, Standards)
Before finalising any presentation output, the agent applies the P-PASS quality filter: Does every slide serve the stated Purpose? Does the structure earn the Persuasion — does it justify the ask? Is the language right for this Audience? Are there Safety risks — unverified claims, reputational exposure, or content that could mislead? Do all slides meet the Standards — consistent headlines, appropriate length, correct format?

The system instruction must also include:
- A detailed description of the agent's professional role and context drawn from my eight answers
- Specific audience calibration rules based on my answer to Question 2
- A slide type library appropriate to my presentation types — with rules for each slide type
- Language and style rules drawn from my tone preference and professional standards
- Knowledge base reference rules — when and how to draw on connected documents
- Non-negotiables drawn from my answer to Question 7
- A clear six-step process the agent follows every time it receives a presentation request:
  Step 1 — Confirm the aim and audience
  Step 2 — Apply ARC to establish the structure
  Step 3 — Propose the slide structure and wait for approval before writing any content
  Step 4 — Write slide content applying OEMGPC to each slide
  Step 5 — Apply P-PASS quality filter before delivering the output
  Step 6 — Deliver the presentation with a brief summary of the ARC applied, OEMGPC logic, and any P-PASS flags for the user to review

Write the instruction in clear, direct professional language. Use headings and numbered sections throughout. The final document must be ready to paste directly into a Microsoft 365 declarative agent without modification.
What to do next

Once the AI has generated your instruction — do this.

1

Read it before you paste it

Scroll through the generated instruction. Check that your professional context, audience, and non-negotiables are accurately reflected. If anything is wrong, correct it in the AI conversation before copying.

2

Run the quality check

Paste the instruction back into the AI and ask: "What is unclear or ambiguous in these instructions? What would you have to guess about rather than know for certain?" Fix what comes up.

3

Save a copy

Copy the full instruction into a Word document or notes app. Name it: M365 Agent System Instruction — [Your Organisation] — June 2026. Always keep a saved version before making changes.

4

Go to Page 2 — the demo and build guide
