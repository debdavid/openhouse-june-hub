Step 1 of 3 · Before you open M365 Copilot

Write your system instruction
using AI.

Before you build your M365 declarative agent, you need a robust system instruction. Use Claude, ChatGPT, or M365 Copilot to generate one — specific to your professional context. This page gives you everything you need to do that.

Why this approach

Don't write your system instruction from scratch.

A good system instruction requires the same rigour as a well-written professional brief — specific, unambiguous, and written as if the agent knows nothing about you. AI will push you to be more specific than you would naturally be on your own.

The quality problem

Most people write vague instructions — "help me create professional presentations" — and wonder why the agent produces generic output. The instruction is the product. Vague in, generic out.

The AI advantage

When you answer eight specific questions, the AI constructs a 4,000 to 8,000 word instruction that incorporates professional communication frameworks, audience calibration, and non-negotiables you would not think to include yourself.

The result

An agent that could not be mistaken for one built for a different professional. Every brief you give it produces structured, purposeful output — calibrated to your context, your audience, your voice.

Built into your instruction

Three frameworks that make every presentation better.

The system instruction generator builds these three frameworks into your agent automatically. You do not need to understand them in detail — your agent will apply them every time it helps you create a presentation.

Framework 1

ARC — Aim, Route, Checkpoints

Your agent always establishes the Aim — the exact outcome the audience must reach — before generating any content. It maps the Route — the narrative path from opening to close. Then it defines 3 to 5 Checkpoints — success criteria that confirm the presentation achieved its aim for this specific audience. All three are confirmed with you before any slide content is written.

Framework 2

OEMGPC — Orient, Explain, Model, Guide, Pre-empt, Commit

Every slide earns its place and moves the audience forward. Orient — frame the context. Explain — deliver the core argument. Model — show the evidence or worked example. Guide — name what this means for this audience specifically. Pre-empt — address the objection they are thinking. Commit — close with the ask and next step. Your agent checks every slide against this sequence before finalising.

Framework 3

P-PASS — Purpose, Persuasion, Audience, Safety, Standards

Before delivering any output, your agent applies this quality filter. Does every slide serve the stated Purpose? Does the structure earn the Persuasion? Is the language right for this Audience? Are there any Safety risks — unverified claims or reputational exposure? Do all slides meet the Standards — consistent headlines, appropriate length, correct format?

The prompt — copy and paste this

Paste this into Claude, ChatGPT, or M365 Copilot.

Answer the eight questions honestly and specifically. The more detail you provide, the better the instruction your agent receives.

How it works: The AI will ask you one question at a time and wait for your answer before moving to the next. At the end, it generates a complete 4,000 to 8,000 word system instruction ready to paste directly into your M365 agent.

Copy this entire prompt — paste into Claude, ChatGPT, or M365 Copilot
I am building a Microsoft 365 declarative agent that will help me create professional presentations. Before you write the system instruction, I need you to understand my professional context. Please ask me the following questions one at a time, wait for my answer, and confirm my response before moving to the next question. Do not skip any question or combine them. QUESTION 1 What is your professional role, and what organisation or business do you work for? Include your industry or sector, the size of your organisation, and where you are based if relevant. QUESTION 2 Who is the primary audience for the presentations this agent will help you create? Describe who is in the room — their professional background, their level of familiarity with your subject matter, and what they need to walk away knowing or deciding. QUESTION 3 What type of presentations will this agent help you create most often? For example: client reports, board updates, funding proposals, team briefings, community presentations, training materials, or something else. Be as specific as possible. QUESTION 4 What tone and communication style best represents your professional voice? Choose from: highly formal and technical, professionally formal, professional and accessible, warm and conversational — or describe your own. QUESTION 5 What are the 2 to 3 key messages, values, or priorities that your presentations should always reflect — regardless of the topic? These might be your organisation's mission, your professional standards, or the outcomes you always want your audience to leave with. QUESTION 6 What documents, templates, or knowledge sources will you connect to this agent? List any files you plan to upload — for example financial reports, policy documents, curriculum standards, product catalogues, or brand guidelines. If none, say none. QUESTION 7 What should this agent never do? Include any language, structure, content, or behaviour that would be unprofessional, inaccurate, or inappropriate for your context. QUESTION 8 What format and structural constraints apply to your presentations? Include slide count limits, required sections, branding requirements, or any other fixed parameters your presentations must meet. --- Once you have all eight answers, write a complete, detailed system instruction for my Microsoft 365 declarative agent. The instruction must: Be between 4,000 and 8,000 words — specific enough that this agent could not be mistaken for one built for a different professional. Incorporate the following three presentation frameworks as governing principles the agent applies to every presentation it helps create: ARC (Aim, Route, Checkpoints) Always establish the Aim before generating any content. Map the Route — the narrative path from opening to close. Define 3 to 5 Checkpoints — success criteria for this specific audience. Confirm all three with the user before writing any slide content. OEMGPC (Orient, Explain, Model, Guide, Pre-empt, Commit) Every slide must earn its place. Orient — frame the context. Explain — deliver the core argument. Model — show evidence or a worked example. Guide — name what this means for this audience. Pre-empt — address the objection they are thinking. Commit — close with the ask and next step. Check every slide against this sequence before finalising. P-PASS (Purpose, Persuasion, Audience, Safety, Standards) Apply this quality filter before delivering any output. Purpose — does every slide serve the stated aim? Persuasion — does the structure earn the ask? Audience — is the language right for this room? Safety — are there any unverified claims or reputational risks? Standards — consistent headlines, appropriate length, correct format throughout. The instruction must also include: - A detailed professional role and context section drawn from my eight answers - Audience calibration rules based on Question 2 - A slide type library appropriate to my presentation types — with rules for each slide type - Language and style rules drawn from my tone preference - Knowledge base reference rules — when and how to draw on uploaded documents - Non-negotiables from Question 7 - A clear step-by-step process the agent follows every time it receives a presentation request: Step 1 — Confirm aim and audience Step 2 — Apply ARC to establish the structure Step 3 — Propose slide structure and wait for approval before writing content Step 4 — Write slide content applying OEMGPC Step 5 — Apply P-PASS quality filter Step 6 — Deliver the output with a brief ARC summary and any P-PASS flags for review Write in clear, professional language with headings and numbered sections throughout. The document should be complete and ready to paste directly into a Microsoft 365 declarative agent without modification.

Before you copy: have your answers to the eight questions roughly in mind before you start. The more specific your answers — particularly Questions 2, 5, and 7 — the more useful the system instruction the AI generates.
